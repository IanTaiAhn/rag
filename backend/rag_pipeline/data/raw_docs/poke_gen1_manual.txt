Section 1 — Overview of the Kanto Region
The Kanto Region is a coastal territory known for its diverse habitats and well‑established Pokémon research centers. Trainers typically begin their journey in small towns and progress toward major cities, each containing a Gym specializing in a particular battle style. The region includes grasslands, caves, forests, and coastal routes, providing a wide range of Pokémon species for study.

Section 2 — Starter Pokémon
Bulbasaur Line
Bulbasaur: A small quadruped Pokémon with a plant bulb on its back. Primary Type: Grass Secondary Type: Poison Evolution: Evolves into Ivysaur at Level 16.

Ivysaur: The bulb begins to bloom as Ivysaur grows stronger. Primary Type: Grass Secondary Type: Poison Evolution: Evolves into Venusaur at Level 32.

Venusaur: A fully developed plant rests on its back, absorbing sunlight for energy. Primary Type: Grass Secondary Type: Poison

Charmander Line
Charmander: A lizard Pokémon with a flame on its tail. Primary Type: Fire Evolution: Evolves into Charmeleon at Level 16.

Charmeleon: Its fiery temperament intensifies as it grows. Primary Type: Fire Evolution: Evolves into Charizard at Level 36.

Charizard: A large winged Pokémon capable of powerful fire attacks. Primary Type: Fire Secondary Type: Flying

Squirtle Line
Squirtle: A small turtle Pokémon known for its defensive shell. Primary Type: Water Evolution: Evolves into Wartortle at Level 16.

Wartortle: Its fluffy tail stores air for underwater stability. Primary Type: Water Evolution: Evolves into Blastoise at Level 36.

Blastoise: Equipped with twin water cannons for high‑pressure attacks. Primary Type: Water

Section 3 — Notable Wild Pokémon
Pikachu
A small electric rodent Pokémon commonly found in forested areas. Primary Type: Electric Behavior: Stores electricity in its cheeks and releases it when threatened.

Geodude
A rock‑like Pokémon found in caves and mountainous terrain. Primary Type: Rock Secondary Type: Ground

Abra
A psychic Pokémon known for its ability to teleport away from danger. Primary Type: Psychic Behavior: Sleeps for most of the day but can sense threats even while resting.

Section 5 — Technical Machines (TMs)
TM01 — Focus Strike
A high‑precision physical attack that increases accuracy when used repeatedly. Type: Normal Power: 70

TM12 — Aqua Pulse
A swirling blast of water that may confuse the target. Type: Water Power: 60

TM22 — Solar Burst
A powerful beam of sunlight that requires one turn to charge. Type: Grass Power: 120

TM34 — Static Shock
A quick electrical strike with a chance to paralyze. Type: Electric Power: 50

Section 6 — Key Items
Poké Flute
A handheld instrument capable of waking sleeping Pokémon. Often used to clear roadblocks caused by large resting Pokémon.

Lift Key
A small metal key used to activate elevators in restricted facilities.

Silph Scope
A specialized device that reveals the true form of ghostly Pokémon.

Bike Voucher
A certificate that can be exchanged for a high‑speed bicycle.

Section 7 — Locations of Interest
Safari Zone
A protected wildlife preserve containing rare Pokémon species. Trainers may enter using a special permit.

Power Plant
An abandoned facility now inhabited by Electric‑type Pokémon. A powerful Legendary Pokémon is rumored to reside within.

Pokémon Tower
A tall structure dedicated to honoring departed Pokémon. Ghost‑type Pokémon are commonly encountered here.
